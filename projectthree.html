Journey

User Experience Design

There are two types of people in this world - those that journal and those that don’t journal. Both types of people face the same challenges of lack of inspiration and not knowing what to write. Journey fixes that by delivering AI curated prompts to each user, probing them to dig deeper into journaling and their own mind.

    Overview

    Problem

    • 1. People don’t feel inspired to journal.
    • 2. Lack of motivation prevents people from journaling.
    • 3. It’s difficult to know what to write with a blank page in front of you.

    Insights

    Most of our respondents said they do not currently keep a journal but have in the past. They cited finding the time to journal as their biggest challenge when trying to maintain a journal. However, when they do sit down to journal, they’ve found that it helps them connect with oneself and not spiral. They’ve tried other means of journaling such as their notes app, GoogleDocs, and prompt journals but nothing seems to compare to a pencil and paper.

    Solutions

    • 1. Create an app that treats journaling like a conversation.
    • 2. Utilize AI to curate individual prompts for each user.
    • 3. Implement interactive features to encourage users to come back to the journal.

    Process

    Assumptions

    I made some general assumptions about people and their experiences journaling.

    • 1. People find it difficult to find time to journal;
    • 2. It’s difficult to know what to write about day to day;

    In our fast-paced world it’s more important than ever to slow down and connect with oneself. That’s why it’s important to a journaling app that can make journaling as easy as having a conversation.

    User Testing

    My team and I conducted 6 user tests to test the functionality of the application.

    Some users thought that the toolbar got in the way of their text while typing. So we instead created a sticky footer that wouldn’t get in the way.

    Some users couldn’t find where to add a new journal. We decided to make that area easier to identify by turning it into an add button instead of a journal.

    Some users said that they were unsure as to whether their entry was saved or not. So we added an autosave alert and save button to alert users that their progress is being saved, and also to let them save to a specific journal.

    Next Steps

    My team and I had a great time working on this project and have lots of ideas for future development. First, to make our journaling app more accessible, we would in the future implement a voice journaling function.

    We would also add additional customizations such as different themes for the app, entry summary generation, and different journaling templates.

    Lessons Learned

    While working on this project we learned that scope-creep is a real thing and that it’s okay to save good ideas for later use.

    We also learned that not everyone loves journaling or is open to the idea of trying it. However, by using prompts it engages the user with the page instead of letting a blank page intimidate them.
