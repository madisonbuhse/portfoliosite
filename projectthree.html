Journey

User Experience Design

There are two types of people in this world - those that journal and those that don’t journal. Both types of people face the same challenges of lack of inspiration and not knowing what to write. Journey fixes that by delivering AI curated prompts to each user, probing them to dig deeper into journaling and their own mind.

    Overview

    Problem

    • 1. People don’t feel inspired to journal.
    • 2. Lack of motivation prevents people from journaling.
    • 3. It’s difficult to know what to write with a blank page in front of you.

    Insights

    Most of our respondents said they do not currently keep a journal but have in the past. They cited finding the time to journal as their biggest challenge when trying to maintain a journal. However, when they do sit down to journal, they’ve found that it helps them connect with oneself and not spiral. They’ve tried other means of journaling such as their notes app, GoogleDocs, and prompt journals but nothing seems to compare to a pencil and paper.

    Solutions

    • 1. Create an app that treats journaling like a conversation.
    • 2. Utilize AI to curate individual prompts for each user.
    • 3. Implement interactive features to encourage users to come back to the journal.

    Process

    Assumptions

    I made some general assumptions about people and their experiences journaling.

    • 1. People find it difficult to find time to journal;
    • 2. It’s difficult to know what to write about day to day;

    In our fast-paced world it’s more important than ever to slow down and connect with oneself. That’s why it’s important to a journaling app that can make journaling as easy as having a conversation.

    Persona

    My teams user interviews gained us very valuable user insights which we then used to form two user personas. The main goal of these personas is to display those patterns and pain points, which then allowed us to further empathize with users.

    Considering both Abby and Kelly’s pain points and goals, the main theme is to give the user direction in what to write about and also provide a way to have a clear mind.

    User Journey Map

    Competitive Analysis Highlights

    To create a unique value proposition for TrailScout, I analyzed our top current competitors. TrailScout offers the main features that our competitors provide to users while our app is more accurate and doesn’t require any extra fees to use.

    User Flow & User Stories

    After conducting market and user research, it’s time to highlight the features of the product. The user stories were used to create User flows, which were done in Figma, to show how the user would interact with the product to accomplish their goals.

    As a new user, I want to sign up and learn about the app and its features.

    As an existing user going on a hike, I want to track my hike and turn on Safety Mode.

    As an existing user that is on a hike with Safety Mode on, I want to end my hike.

    User Testing

    My team and I conducted 6 user tests to test the functionality of the application.

    Some users wondered if their emergency contacts would be saved or not, so we added a feature where you can choose from existing contacts and also add more.

    Some users wondered about what the messages sent from the app would say. So we decided to include a preview of the message that would be sent when you begin a trail, are in an emergency, and when you finish a trail.

    Next Steps

    Next, we would update the “Nature Facts” tab with helpful information about plants and animals that may be encountered on trails.

    We would also develop a “Community” tab/feature where users could connect with others and form their own groups.

    Lessons Learned

    While working on this project we learned that scope-creep is a real thing and that it’s okay to save good ideas for later use.

    We also learned that people love the outdoors but find it difficult to find new spots to explore that are safe and accessible for their skill level. Meaning it’s more important than ever to have recommended trails and accurate maps.
